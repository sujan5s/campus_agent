Section 05 · Set AAutomated Timetable Scheduling (CP-SAT / OR-Tools)

University timetabling is a classic NP-hard constraint satisfaction problem. We solve it with Google OR-Tools CP-SAT — a constraint solver — not an LLM.

PaperSource / YearCore IdeaLimitation / GapWhat We Take
A Comparative Analysis of Constraint Programming and Genetic Algorithms for Automated University Timetable GenerationIEEE, 2024Compares Google CP-SAT vs a Genetic Algorithm; CP is ~175× faster and functionally superior on a realistic problem.Benchmark study, not a product.Direct justification for choosing OR-Tools CP-SAT over GA — our headline design citation.
Genetic Algorithm for Solving University Course Timetabling Using Dynamic ChromosomesIEEE, 2021GA with dynamic-length chromosomes for course timetabling.Stochastic; convergence and tuning issues; no optimality guarantee.Baseline we compare against; motivates a deterministic solver.
Time Table Scheduling Using Genetic Algorithms Employing Guided MutationIEEE, 2011Guided-mutation GA avoids faculty / classroom clashes.Quality depends on operators; slow on large instances.Confirms constraint types (faculty / room clash) we encode as hard constraints.
Solving Timetabling Problems Using Genetic AlgorithmsIEEE, 2004Foundational GA formulation of timetabling.Older; small-scale; no infeasibility explanation.Establishes timetabling as an evolutionary-search problem in the literature.
Solving University Timetabling as a Constraint Satisfaction Problem with Genetic AlgorithmScholarModels timetabling explicitly as a CSP.Hybrid; still stochastic.Confirms the CSP formulation we adopt for our CP-SAT model.
Automating University Course Scheduling Using Genetic AlgorithmSpringer, 2025Recent GA-based course scheduling with a practical framework.GA limitations persist: runtime, no explanation.Recent evidence the field still leans on GA — our CP-SAT + explanation is a step beyond.
How this maps to our project
Our app/solver/ module builds an OR-Tools CP-SAT model with boolean assignment variables, hard constraints for clash-freeness and period counts, and soft objectives for load balance. The comparison paper (IEEE 11379218) empirically shows CP-SAT is ~175× faster than a GA, which is precisely why we reject the GA-heavy mainstream. Our added value: when the problem is infeasible, the LLM explains which constraints conflict and suggests relaxations — a usability capability none of these papers provide.

Section 11 · Set BDifferentiation — What Makes This Project Stand Out

The gap in existing work, and how our approach is novel. This section supports the novelty / contribution slide and the gap-analysis chapter.

The one-line thesis

Existing campus systems are reactive (IoT dashboards), and existing agentic-AI research is generic or applied to other domains (cloud, parking, railways). No prior system combines autonomous multi-agent orchestration, proactive event-driven action, and human-in-the-loop approval, applied to integrated campus operations. That combination is our contribution.

The four pillars of our novelty

Proactive, not reactive. Agents act on events (leave approved → reschedule) and schedules (nightly checks) with no human prompt. Smart-campus literature is explicitly reactive by its own framing.
Right tool for each job. Constraint solver for timetables, RAG for knowledge, structured-output LLM for routing — the LLM orchestrates, it does not guess. Timetabling research shows LLM-free CP-SAT is the correct engineering choice.
Safe autonomy via human-in-the-loop. The system proposes; humans approve high-impact actions in one click.
Integrated, single platform. One agentic system spans scheduling, substitution, booking, and knowledge — prior work solves these in isolation.

Report-ready formulation

“While agentic AI has been surveyed extensively and applied to domains such as cloud support and smart parking, and while smart-campus research is mature in IoT sensing, the two have not been combined. Existing campus systems remain reactive and siloed, timetabling research relies largely on slower stochastic genetic algorithms without infeasibility reasoning, and teacher-substitution work stops at classification. Our system unifies these into a single agentic platform that acts proactively on events, uses a constraint solver with LLM-generated explanations for timetabling, and keeps humans in control through explicit approval checkpoints — a combination not present in the reviewed literature.”
